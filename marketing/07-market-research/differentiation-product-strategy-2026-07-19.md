## Primary Customer To Win

### Primary wedge: event and community PIC

Internal evidence makes this the strongest starting segment:

- most frequent order segment;
- highest stated revenue segment, typically Rp10 juta-Rp30 juta;
- best stated margin segment;
- repeat behavior exists;
- first customer survey describes a 1,000-piece event order completed on time;
- the decision maker is usually the person responsible for the organization or event.

The order value and 1,000-piece case remain internal facts until owner approval and publication permission are obtained.

### Secondary wedge: vendor and reseller

This segment repeats, already buys kaos custom and manual screen printing, and benefits directly from reusable specifications. It is attractive only after tier, confidentiality, capacity, QC, and channel-conflict rules are clear.

### Supporting lane: individual and test order

Single-unit DTF remains useful as an entry point, sample path, and local demand capture. It should not define the whole brand because owner data says this segment is more price-sensitive, often lacks a finished design, and cancels more frequently.

## Customer Job To Be Done

### Event / community

When I am responsible for apparel for an event or group, help me convert an incomplete design, member sizes, budget, and fixed date into an approved production plan, so I can deliver the right items without repeatedly chasing the vendor or risking an embarrassing mistake.

### Vendor / reseller

When a customer asks for a repeat or similar production, help me retrieve the known specification and get a reliable quotation quickly, so I do not re-explain the order and can protect my time, margin, and customer relationship.

### Individual / test order

When I have one design but do not understand fabrics or printing methods, help me choose a workable option and see the expected result before spending more.

The emotional and social outcomes above are product hypotheses inferred from the functional evidence. They need customer interviews, lost-lead interviews, and order observation before being treated as facts.

## Competitive Reality

### What competitors already own publicly

1. TKS Factory: reduced production anxiety through `No Drama`, transparency, tracking, longevity, and corporate proof.
2. Teespace: convenience through catalogue commerce, price visibility, social proof signals, guarantee, and tracking.
3. Rumah Sablon Cepat: local high-intent capture through price, MOQ, location, and speed.
4. Seragam Bangsa: procurement readiness through direct production, samples, invoice, workshop evidence, and QC.
5. Ciptaloka: fast self-service for standard one-piece custom products.
6. Larger production specialists: sample approval and production-control content.

### What is not a differentiator for Orplyn

- `murah` or `harga kompetitif`;
- `berkualitas`;
- `cepat` or `tepat waktu` without an SLA and proof;
- `bisa satuan sampai ribuan`;
- `konsultasi gratis`;
- `banyak pilihan teknik`;
- `pengiriman seluruh Indonesia`;
- `workshop di Ciputat` by itself.

These remain useful buying requirements and SEO facts. They should be supported, but they cannot carry the brand position alone.

## Orplyn's Real Starting Assets

1. Confirmed product rules across one-piece and batch orders.
2. A broad real-production asset archive across DTF, manual printing, special effects, embroidery, jersey, fullprint, and merchandise.
3. Customer evidence that consultation helped resolve material and printing choices.
4. Event/community and vendor segments with stated repeat behavior.

## Chosen Product Thesis

### Category

Custom apparel and print production for event/community, business, partner, and individual needs.

### Differentiation wedge

`Spec-first, repeat-ready production.`

### Product promise to build

Before production begins, Orplyn records and confirms:

- intended use;
- product and material;
- quantity and size breakdown;
- artwork version;
- print or embroidery method;
- placement and size;
- approved preview or sample reference;
- target completion date;
- delivery or pickup;
- commercial terms.

After completion, the final specification is retained under an Order ID so a repeat order can start from the approved reference instead of a blank chat.

### Why a qualified buyer should choose Orplyn

Choose Orplyn when the main risk is not merely finding someone who can print, but making sure the product, technique, artwork, quantity, and date are understood before production and easy to reproduce later.

Do not choose Orplyn solely because you want the lowest advertised price, an instant self-service editor, or a public tracking portal. Competitors currently show stronger proof for those jobs.

## Product System To Build

Internal working name: `Orplyn Production Record`.

### P0 - Guided brief

Status: partly implemented.

- Lead ID;
- use case;
- product;
- quantity;
- deadline;
- design status;
- material preference;
- delivery location;
- attribution.

Next improvement: send the data into an operational lead log, not only WhatsApp.

### P0 - Comparable recommendation

For qualified requests, admin provides at most three sensible options:

- budget-conscious option;
- recommended fit;
- upgraded option when relevant.

Every option must state material, technique, included scope, MOQ, production estimate, and price/range. This requires M-07 owner approval.

### P0 - Production specification and approval

Create a customer-readable order specification with:

- Order ID and quotation version;
- final product and quantities;
- size/color breakdown;
- artwork thumbnail and version;
- print size and placement;
- material and technique;
- target date and delivery;
- price, DP, and balance;
- customer approval timestamp;
- notes and exclusions.

No production status may move to `Approved for production` without explicit approval evidence.

### P1 - Milestone updates

Use simple WhatsApp templates first:

1. details received;
2. quotation/spec sent;
3. design/spec approved and DP received;
4. production started;
5. QC/finishing;
6. ready for pickup or shipping.

Do not build a customer portal until manual milestone updates prove customers value them and the team can maintain accurate status.

### P1 - QC and closeout record

Define the owner-approved checks and rework rule. Record final quantity, issue count, correction, completion date, and delivery proof. Public quality claims require actual operating evidence.

### P1 - Repeat order

Allow admin to retrieve the prior Order ID, duplicate the approved specification, change only quantity/date/size breakdown, re-quote, and obtain fresh approval.

### P2 - Partner workflow

After direct orders work reliably, test partner fields such as partner ID, confidentiality, blind shipping, end-customer reference, partner price tier, and channel-conflict rule.

3. event/community case study with quantity, challenge, selected solution, and deadline outcome;

## Website And Sales Implication

Do not rewrite the public hero around the aspirational differentiation yet. The current literal category/location offer is safer until workflow proof exists.

After validation, the message hierarchy can become:

1. Category: sablon and custom apparel by Orplyn.
2. Audience: event, community, business, and repeat partner.
3. Outcome: production details clear before starting.
4. Mechanism: guided recommendation, approved specification, and saved reorder reference.
5. Proof: real order, real result, real review, and process artifact.

The CTA can remain `Minta estimasi`. The next screen should eventually return a recommendation or specification preview, not only open a generic chat.

## Validation Plan

### Stage 1 - Discovery

- interview 5 event/community PICs;
- interview 3 repeat vendor/reseller customers;
- interview 3 lost leads;
- ask what caused the most uncertainty, rework, delay, or repeated explanation;
- rank price, result fit, deadline, communication, and reorder convenience.

### Stage 2 - Concierge pilot

Run five real orders manually with the production-record workflow. Do not build software first.

Measure:

- number of clarification rounds before quote;
- quote turnaround time;
- missing fields at approval;
- changes after approval;
- production issue and rework reason;
- on-time outcome;
- customer confidence feedback;
- time required for a repeat quotation;
- quote, sale, revenue, and gross profit.

### Stage 3 - Positioning test

Compare two message directions on qualified traffic or outbound:

- Control: `sablon custom dari satuan sampai batch`;
- Challenger: `spesifikasi jelas sebelum produksi, tersimpan untuk repeat order`.

Primary outcome: qualified lead to sale and gross profit, not click-through rate alone.

## Decision

1. Keep price, proof, review, SLA, capacity, and QC claims gated.
2. Treat one-piece-to-batch capability as a useful range, not a unique differentiator.
3. Focus product discovery on event/community PICs and repeat partners.
4. Build differentiation through production clarity and repeatability.
5. Start with a spreadsheet/document concierge workflow before custom software.
6. Promote the positioning publicly only after owner approval and pilot evidence.
