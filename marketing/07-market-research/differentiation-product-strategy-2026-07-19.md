## Primary Customer To Win

### Primary wedge: event and community PIC

Internal evidence makes this the strongest starting segment:

- most frequent order segment;
- highest stated revenue segment, typically Rp10 juta-Rp30 juta;
- best stated margin segment;
- repeat behavior exists;
- first customer survey describes a 1,000-piece event order completed on time;
- the decision maker is usually the person responsible for the organization or event.

The order value and 1,000-piece case remain internal facts until owner approval and publication permission are obtained.

### Deferred secondary segment: vendor and reseller

This segment repeats, already buys kaos custom and manual screen printing, and may benefit from reusable specifications. It is not a parallel acquisition focus during the current event/community concentration. Revisit it only after event baseline, tier, confidentiality, capacity, QC, and channel-conflict rules are clear.

### Supporting lane: individual and test order

Single-unit DTF remains useful as an entry point, sample path, and local demand capture. It is not a primary campaign during the current event/community concentration and should not define the whole brand because owner data says this segment is more price-sensitive, often lacks a finished design, and cancels more frequently.

## Customer Job To Be Done

### Event / community

When I am responsible for apparel for an event or group, help me convert an incomplete design, member sizes, budget, and fixed date into an approved production plan, so I can deliver the right items without repeatedly chasing the vendor or risking an embarrassing mistake.

### Vendor / reseller

When a customer asks for a repeat or similar production, help me retrieve the known specification and get a reliable quotation quickly, so I do not re-explain the order and can protect my time, margin, and customer relationship.

### Individual / test order

When I have one design but do not understand fabrics or printing methods, help me choose a workable option and see the expected result before spending more.

The emotional and social outcomes above are product hypotheses inferred from the functional evidence. They need customer interviews, lost-lead interviews, and order observation before being treated as facts.

## Evolved Product Thesis

### Category

Custom apparel and print production for event/community, business, partner, and individual needs.

### Acquisition hypothesis for first order

`Help the event PIC assess and control deadline risk from the event date backward.`

This is H-009, not a public promise. The mechanism to test is date-first intake, feasibility, cut-off, risk flag, approval timing, and actual completion evidence on five real event orders through E-12.

### Retention hypothesis

`Spec-first, repeat-ready production.`

This is H-008. It may reduce repeated explanation and requote effort only after a customer has an approved prior order. It is not the lead acquisition headline.

### Retention mechanism to build

Before production begins, Orplyn records and confirms:

- intended use;
- product and material;
- quantity and size breakdown;
- artwork version;
- print or embroidery method;
- placement and size;
- approved preview or sample reference;
- target completion date;
- delivery or pickup;
- commercial terms.

After completion, the final specification is retained under an Order ID so a repeat order can start from the approved reference instead of a blank chat.

### Candidate reason a qualified buyer might choose Orplyn

For a first event order, test whether the buyer values a vendor that starts from the event date and makes feasibility, approval, cut-off, and production risk explicit. For a repeat order, test whether the saved specification makes the next order materially easier.

Neither statement is proven. Competitors currently show stronger public proof for price visibility, guarantees, tracking, and self-service convenience, so Orplyn must earn the deadline and repeat-order claims through operating evidence.

## Product Systems To Test

Acquisition mechanism: date-first event feasibility and risk triage.

Retention mechanism, internal working name: `Orplyn Production Record`.

### Acquisition P0 - Date-first event intake

For event/community leads, record and review:

- event date and days-to-event;
- intended use and delivery location;
- product, quantity, size complexity, and design/spec status;
- capacity and cut-off rule used;
- feasibility decision: feasible, adjust scope/date, or decline;
- risk note and responsible PIC;
- approval/DP deadline and target completion.

This workflow is internal during E-12. It does not authorize a public deadline or slot promise.

### Shared P0 - Guided brief and lead log

Status: partly implemented.

- Lead ID;
- use case;
- product;
- quantity;
- deadline;
- design status;
- material preference;
- delivery location;
- attribution.

Next improvement: send the data into an operational lead log, not only WhatsApp.

### P0 - Comparable recommendation

For qualified requests, admin provides at most three sensible options:

- budget-conscious option;
- recommended fit;
- upgraded option when relevant.

Every option must state material, technique, included scope, MOQ, production estimate, and price/range. This requires M-07 owner approval.

### P0 - Production specification and approval

Create a customer-readable order specification with:

- Order ID and quotation version;
- final product and quantities;
- size/color breakdown;
- artwork thumbnail and version;
- print size and placement;
- material and technique;
- target date and delivery;
- price, DP, and balance;
- customer approval timestamp;
- notes and exclusions.

No production status may move to `Approved for production` without explicit approval evidence.

### P1 - Milestone updates

Use simple WhatsApp templates first:

1. details received;
2. quotation/spec sent;
3. design/spec approved and DP received;
4. production started;
5. QC/finishing;
6. ready for pickup or shipping.

Do not build a customer portal until manual milestone updates prove customers value them and the team can maintain accurate status.

### P1 - QC and closeout record

Define the owner-approved checks and rework rule. Record final quantity, issue count, correction, completion date, and delivery proof. Public quality claims require actual operating evidence.

### P1 - Repeat order

Allow admin to retrieve the prior Order ID, duplicate the approved specification, change only quantity/date/size breakdown, re-quote, and obtain fresh approval.

### P2 - Partner workflow

After direct orders work reliably, test partner fields such as partner ID, confidentiality, blind shipping, end-customer reference, partner price tier, and channel-conflict rule.

## Validation Plan

### Stage 1 - Top-of-funnel and measurement minimum

- establish a lead log and verify the owner baseline of 2-5 new chats per week;
- complete GBP/NAP and authentic review work;
- publish only permissioned event production proof without deadline outcome claims;
- test event/customer referrals and focused local event outreach;
- keep event/community results separate from DTF single and other segments.

### Stage 2 - E-12 event deadline-risk pilot

Run five real event orders manually after capacity, cut-off, commercial rules, and PIC ownership are approved. Start from the event date, but do not make a public safety or on-time promise.

Measure:

- source, response time, and days-to-event;
- feasibility and accept/reject decision;
- quote turnaround time;
- missing fields at approval;
- changes after approval;
- production issue and rework reason;
- target and actual completion;
- on-time outcome;
- PIC feedback about deadline/order risk;
- quote, sale, revenue, and gross profit.

### Stage 3 - E-11 repeat-ready pilot

For eligible completed orders and repeat opportunities, save the approved specification and measure clarification rounds, changed fields, repeat requote time, repeat sale, revenue, gross profit, and customer feedback. Do not infer retention benefit from a first order alone.

### Stage 4 - Positioning test

Only after the relevant gate passes, compare a factual control with one approved challenger at a time. Candidate directions remain hypotheses:

- Control: `sablon custom dari satuan sampai batch`;
- Acquisition challenger: date-first event/deadline-risk framing supported by E-12;
- Retention challenger: saved specification/repeat-order framing supported by E-11.

Primary outcome: qualified lead to sale and gross profit, not click-through rate alone.

## Decision

1. Keep price, proof, review, SLA, capacity, and QC claims gated.
2. Treat one-piece-to-batch capability as a useful range, not a unique differentiator.
3. Concentrate acquisition on event/community and top-of-funnel volume before further funnel polishing.
4. Test deadline-risk handling as H-009/E-12 for first-order acquisition.
5. Use spec-first/repeat-ready only as H-008/E-11 for retention.
6. Start with a spreadsheet/document concierge workflow before custom software.
7. Promote either positioning publicly only after its own evidence, permission, owner approval, and Public-Claim Gate pass.
